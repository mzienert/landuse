Sec. 54-31. Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article,
shall
have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County roads
means all public highways on the county road system as shown on the map submitted to the state department of transportation pursuant to C.R.S. § 43-2-120.
Effective on: 4/22/2014
Emergency stopping
or parking only means when official signs are erected, giving notice thereof, no person shall stop, stand or park a vehicle on the shoulder of any highway or any other facility so marked except in case of an emergency involving the vehicle or its occupants.
Effective on: 4/22/2014
Loading zone
means a designated space reserved for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of passengers or property.
Effective on: 4/22/2014
Park
or parking means the standing of a vehicle, whether occupied or not, otherwise than very briefly for the purpose of and while engaged in loading or unloading property or passengers.
Effective on: 4/22/2014
Snow route
means roads designated with official snow route signs, on which vehicles shall not be parked so as to impede snow removal operations which are imminent or in progress.
Effective on: 4/22/2014
Stand
or standing means the halting of a vehicle, whether occupied or not, other than momentarily for the purpose of and while actually engaged in receiving or discharging passengers.
Effective on: 4/22/2014
Stop
or stopping means, when prohibited, any halting, even momentarily, of a vehicle, whether occupied or not, except when necessary to avoid conflict with other traffic or in compliance with the directions of a police officer or official traffic control device.
Effective on: 4/22/2014
Vehicle
means any device which is capable of moving itself, or being moved, from place to place upon wheels or endless tracks. Such term shall include any farm tractor or any implement of husbandry designed primarily or exclusively for use and used in agricultural operations, and such term shall not mean or include any device if moved by muscular power, or moved exclusively over stationary rails or tracks, or designed to move primarily through the air.
(Ord. No. 2014-1, § 3(Exh. A), 4-22-2014)

Sec. 54-57. Authority for immobilization and impoundment.
When a driver,
owner
or
person
in charge of a
vehicle
has failed to respond to a notice of illegal parking and has failed to respond to the additional notice sent to the registered owner, deputies of the
county
sheriff
may
and are hereby authorized to temporarily, and for a period of 72 hours, immobilize such vehicle by installing or attaching to such vehicle a device designated to restrict the normal movement of such vehicle. If a vehicle is so immobilized, the deputy sheriff installing or attaching such device
shall
conspicuously affix to such vehicle a notice in writing on form provided by the county, advising the owner, driver or person in charge of such vehicle that such vehicle has been immobilized by the county for
violation
of one or more of the above referenced provisions. Such notice shall also advise the owner, driver or person in charge of such vehicle that release from such immobilization may be obtained at a designated place and that unless arrangements are made for the release from immobilization of such vehicle within 72 hours, the vehicle will be removed from the county road at the direction of a deputy sheriff. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove or attempt to remove any such device before a release is obtained as provided in this section. If the vehicle has remained immobilized for a period of 72 hours or more and release has not been obtained within such period, or if the vehicle must be removed for purposes of road maintenance or snow removal, a deputy sheriff or a county employee shall have the authority to have such vehicle impounded.
(Ord. No. 2014-1, § 3(Exh. A), 4-22-2014)
Effective on: 4/22/2014

Sec. 54-61. Penalty assessment procedure.
I.
Penalty assessment notice. When a
person
is arrested for an offense as set forth in C.R.S § 30-15-402, as amended, the arresting officer
shall
issue the person a penalty assessment notice and release the person upon its terms. The penalty assessment notice, except as otherwise provided for in this section regarding illegally parked
vehicles
, shall be a summons and complaint containing identification of the alleged offender, specification of the offense and applicable fine, a requirement that the alleged offender pay the fine or appear to answer the charge at a specified time and place, and other matters reasonably adopted effectuating the purpose of this article.
II.
Acknowledgement or denial of guilt. If the person given a penalty assessment notice chooses to acknowledge his guilt, he
may
pay the specified fine in person or by mail at the place and within the time specified in the notice. If he chooses not to acknowledge his guilt, the defendant shall appear as required in the notice. Upon trial, if the alleged offender is found guilty, the fine imposed shall be that specified in the notice for the offense of which he was found guilty, but customary court costs may be assessed against him in
addition
to the fine.
III.
Notice on illegally parked vehicle. Whenever any motor vehicle without a driver is found parked or stopped in
violation
of any of the restrictions imposed by this article, the law enforcement officer finding such vehicle shall take its license plate number and state of registration of the vehicle, and may take any other information displayed on the vehicle which may identify its user, and shall conspicuously affix to such vehicle a penalty assessment notice, indicating the offense or infraction and directing the
owner or operator
of the vehicle to remit the penalty assessment provided for by this article for the offense or infraction specified, directing the driver thereof to respond to and answer the charge against him at a place and at a time specified in the notice.
IV.
Failure to comply with notice on parked vehicle. If the driver or
owner
of an unattended motor vehicle charged with an apparent violation of the restrictions on stopping, standing or parking under this article does not respond within the time specified to a penalty assessment notice affixed to such vehicle, as provided for in this section, by appearance and payment at the
county
court, or by mailing payment by means of the United States mail, or by other disposition of the charge as provided by law, the clerk of the county court shall send another notice by mail to the registered owner of the vehicle to which the original notice was affixed, warning him that if such notice is disregarded for a period of 20 days from date of mailing, a complaint will be filed and a warrant of arrest will be issued.
V.
Presumption in reference to illegal parking. In any prosecution charging a violation of any provision of this article governing the stopping, standing or parking of a vehicle, proof that the particular vehicle described in the complaint was parked in violation of this article, together with proof that the defendant named in the complaint was at the time of such parking the registered owner of such vehicle, shall constitute in evidence a prima facie presumption that the registered owner of such vehicle was the person who parked or placed such vehicle at the point where, and for the time during which, such violation occurred.
(Ord. No. 2014-1, § 3(Exh. A), 4-22-2014;
Ord. No.
2022-01
, § 54-61 (Exh. F), 09/27/2022
)
Effective on: 9/27/2022

Sec. 54-74. Penalty assessment procedure.
I.
Penalty assessment notice. When a
person
is arrested for an offense as set forth in C.R.S. § 30-15-402, as amended, the parking enforcement officer
shall
issue the person a penalty assessment notice and release the person upon its terms. The penalty assessment notice, except as otherwise provided for in this section regarding illegally parked
vehicles
, shall be a summons and complaint containing identification of the alleged offender, specification of the offense and applicable fine, a requirement that the alleged offender pay the fine or appear to answer the charge at a specified time and place, and other matters reasonably adopted effectuating the purpose of this article.
II.
Acknowledgement or denial of guilt. If the person given a penalty assessment notice chooses to acknowledge his guilt, he
may
pay the specified fine in person, by mail or other available payment methods within the time specified in the notice. If he chooses not to acknowledge his guilt, the defendant shall appear as required in the notice. Upon trial, if the alleged offender is found guilty, the fine imposed shall be that specified in the notice for the offense of which he was found guilty along with any customary court costs, which shall be in
addition
to the fine.
III.
Notice on illegally parked vehicle. Whenever any motor vehicle without a driver is found parked or stopped in
violation
of any of the restrictions imposed by this article, the parking enforcement officer finding such vehicle shall take its license plate number and state of registration of the vehicle, and may take any other information displayed on the vehicle which may identify its user, and shall conspicuously affix to such vehicle a penalty assessment notice, which shall indicate the offense or infraction and direct the
owner or operator
of the vehicle to either remit the penalty assessment provided for by this article for the offense or infraction specified along with applicable fine, or to respond to and answer the charge against him at a place and at a time specified in the notice.
IV.
Failure to comply with notice on parked vehicle. If the driver or
owner
of a motor vehicle charged with an apparent violation of the restrictions on stopping, standing or parking under this article does not respond to a penalty assessment notice affixed to such vehicle, as provided for in this section, by paying the applicable fine either in person, by mail or other available payment methods within the time specified in the notice, the penalty assessment notice shall be transmitted to the clerk of the
county
court as a summons. The summons shall be set for a hearing in county court at the next regularly scheduled date on which the court handles such infractions. The driver or owner of the vehicle may then pay the fine set forth in the penalty assessment notice along with all applicable court costs or request to contest the penalty assessment notice. If the driver or owner of the unattended vehicle chooses to contest the penalty assessment, the court will set a date for the trial.
V.
Presumption in reference to illegal parking. In any prosecution charging a violation of any provision of this article governing the stopping, standing or parking of a vehicle, proof that the particular vehicle described in the complaint was parked in violation of this article, together with proof that the defendant named in the complaint was at the time of such parking the registered owner of such vehicle, shall constitute in evidence a prima facie presumption that the registered owner of such vehicle was the person who parked or placed such vehicle at the point where, and for the time during which, such violation occurred.

Sec. 54-131. Definition.
As used in this division, the term "
person
with a disability" means as provided for in C.R.S. § 42-3-121(1).
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10, 9-8-1997)
Cross reference—
Definitions generally, §
1-2.
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 54-132. Signed or identified vehicles permitted to park.
In a
jurisdiction
recognizing the privilege defined by section 54-133, a
vehicle
with distinguishing license plates or an identifying placard
may
be parked in public
parking areas
or along public streets, when complying with regulations imposed by official signs designating specialized zones.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10-2, 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 54-133. Identification requirements.
Vehicle
of disabled
person
. A person with a disability
may
park
in a parking space identified as being reserved for
use
by persons with disabilities whether on public property or private property available for public use. A placard or license plate issued to a person with a disability
shall
be displayed on the vehicle while parked in such space.
Reservation of space by property
owner
; enforcement by peace officers. The owner of private property available for public use may reserve parking spaces for use by persons with disabilities. Such an
action
shall be a waiver of any objection the owner may assert concerning enforcement of this section by peace officers of any political
subdivision
of this state, and such officers are hereby authorized and empowered to so enforce this section, provisions of law to the contrary notwithstanding.
Official sign identifying parking space. Each parking space reserved for use by persons with disabilities whether on public property or private property shall be marked with an official upright sign, which sign may be stationary or portable, identifying such parking space as reserved for use by persons with disabilities.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10-3, 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 54-134. Use of space by out-of-state disabled persons.
Persons
with disabilities from states other than Colorado
shall
be allowed to
use
parking spaces for persons with disabilities in Colorado so long as such persons have valid license plates or placards from their home state.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10-4, 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 54-135. Parking in reserved areas by those not disabled.
It is unlawful for any
person
other than a person with a disability to
park
in a parking space on
public or private property
which is clearly identified by an official sign as being reserved for
use
by persons with disabilities, unless such person is parking the
vehicle
for the benefit of a person with a disability.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10-5, 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 54-136. Violation of division an offense.
Any
person
who is not a person with a disability and who exercises the privilege defined in section 54-132 or who violates the provisions of section 54-137 commits a traffic offense.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10-6, 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 54-137. Improper use of license plate or placard an offense.
Any
person
who is not a person with a disability and who uses a license plate or placard issued to a person with a disability pursuant to C.R.S. § 42-3-121(2) in order to receive the benefits or privileges available to a person with a disability under this division commits a traffic offense.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10-7, 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 54-138. Checking of identification of user by law enforcement officer authorized.
Any law enforcement officer or authorized parking enforcement official
may
check the identification of any
person
using a license plate or placard for persons with disabilities in order to determine whether such
use
is authorized.
(Ord. No. 1997-3, § 6.10-8, 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Secs. 54-139—54-170. Reserved.
Effective on: 9/8/1997
