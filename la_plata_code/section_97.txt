Sec. 2-301. Creation; composition; appointments.
There is hereby created the extension advisory committee (formerly known as the extension advisory
board
) to be comprised of 11 members appointed by, and serving at the pleasure of, the
board of county commissioners
. In appointing these members, the board of
county
commissioners
shall
attempt to achieve demographic and geographic representation, as well as representation from various user groups of extension office services.
(Res. No. 1997-11, § 1, 2-10-1997)
Effective on: 2/10/1997
Sec. 2-302. Terms.
Appointees
shall
serve staggered terms of three years each.
(Res. No. 1997-11, § 2, 2-10-1997)
Effective on: 2/10/1997
Sec. 2-303. Succession.
Members of the extension advisory committee
shall
serve until their successors are appointed.
(Res. No. 1997-11, § 3, 2-10-1997)
Effective on: 2/10/1997
Sec. 2-304. Filling of vacancies.
Vacancies
shall
be filled by special appointment made by the
board
of
county
commissioners
. Such appointment shall be to fill the unexpired term of the vacant member's seat.
(Res. No. 1997-11, § 4, 2-10-1997)
Effective on: 2/10/1997
Sec. 2-305. Qualifications of members.
All members must be residents of the
county
.
(Res. No. 1997-11, § 5, 2-10-1997; Res. No. 2010-44, § 1, 9-14-2010)
Effective on: 9/14/2010
Sec. 2-306. Election of officers; meetings.
The extension advisory committee members
shall
elect from their membership a chair, vice-chair and any other officers they deem appropriate for the accomplishment of their task. Meetings of the extension advisory committee shall be open to the public and conducted in accordance with the Colorado Sunshine Act, C.R.S. § 24-6-401 et seq. Notices and agendas of public meetings of the extension advisory committee shall be posted at the La Plata
County
Administration Building located at 1101 East Second Avenue, Durango, Colorado. The extension advisory committee shall meet as often as necessary to fulfill its responsibilities.
(Res. No. 1997-11, § 6, 2-10-1997)
Effective on: 10/25/2016
Sec. 2-307. Quorum; duties.
I.
The presence of a majority of the extension advisory committee
shall
constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Such extension advisory committee shall have the duties as provided in this division and shall act in an advisory capacity to the
board
of
county
commissioners
.
II.
The extension advisory committee shall work with the local extension office personnel, in cooperation with the state cooperative extension service, in planning, developing, implementing and evaluating programs and performance.
(Res. No. 1997-11, §§ 7, 8, 2-10-1997)
Effective on: 2/10/1997
