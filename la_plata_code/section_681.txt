Sec. 42-406. Enumeration of specific requirements for extra-legal vehicles.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load
may
travel on
county
highways 24 hours per day, seven days per week, except as follows:
An extra-legal vehicle or load more than 14 feet in width is prohibited from travel during hours of darkness, unless authorized under a
special permit
.
An extra-legal vehicle or load is prohibited from travel when:
The
department
, state patrol or other peace officer determines and provides public notice by any available means that a hazardous road condition exists for an extra-legal vehicle or load; or
The
permittee
knows that a hazardous road condition exists for an extra-legal vehicle or load. Hazardous road conditions may include:
Water, ice, snow, mud, wind, or rocks on the highway.
Debris from an accident, natural disaster, or
emergency
on the highway.
If a hazardous condition exists on the route to be traveled, the permittee (not applicable to annual
permit
holders)
shall
comply with the following procedure to determine if an alternate route is available:
The permittee shall take the permit to the department.
The department must be contacted to determine if an alternate route is available.
If the department determines that an alternate route is available, the permit must be changed by department personnel.
If the department cannot be contacted or if the department determines that an alternate route is not available, then no changes shall be made to the permit and the extra-legal vehicle or load shall not travel until the hazardous condition ceases to exist.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-1), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-407. Drive in right lane.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load which is 13 feet or more in width
shall
travel only in the farthest righthand lane available to traffic and shall travel as close as practicable to the righthand curb or painted shoulder line, except when passing another vehicle or preparing for a left turn.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-2), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-408. Divisible extra-legal vehicles.
A divisible extra-legal
vehicle
or load is prohibited from travel on
county
highways, except as provided in divisions 2 and 3 of this article.

, § 42-408, 04/09/2019
)

Sec. 42-409. Prohibited routes.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load with axle configurations which exceed the
maximum limits
for axle weight for certain highways, as described on the
bridge weight limit map
, or prohibited overweight route list is prohibited from travel on such highways unless authorized under either a
special permit
.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-4), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-410. Crossing bridges.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load between 140,000 pounds and 200,000 pounds gross vehicle weight must:
Not exceed 20 miles per hour when crossing bridges; and
Travel in the center of the driving lane when crossing bridges.
An extra-legal vehicle or load more than 200,000 pounds gross vehicle weight and all special mobile machinery must:
Not exceed ten miles per hour when crossing bridges;
Travel in the center of the driving lane when crossing bridges; and
Attach to the rear of the extra-legal vehicle or load an Oversize Load Sign reading as follows:
"CAUTION: THIS VEHICLE MUST SLOW TO 10 MPH TO CROSS ALL BRIDGES."
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-5), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-411. Signs required.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load
shall
display "Wide Load," "Long Load," or "Oversize Load" signs, as applicable, in the extreme front and rear of the extra-legal vehicle or load, except as provided in division 2 of this article. All such signs shall either be five feet wide, ten inches high with one-inch wide brush stroke, black letters a minimum of eight inches high on yellow background; or shall be seven feet wide, 18 inches high, with a 1.41-inch brush stroke, black letters a minimum of ten inches high on yellow background.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-6), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-412. Minimum distances.
A minimum distance of one-half mile
shall
be maintained at all times between extra-legal
vehicles
or load, except when passing or when otherwise authorized under a
special permit
. One extra-legal vehicle or load
may
pass another extra-legal vehicle or load only under the following conditions:
There must be no other traffic in the immediate vicinity on the
county
highway; and
The vehicles or loads involved must communicate by radio concerning the pass prior to making the passing maneuver.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-7), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-413. Modular/mobile home sheathing.
A load consisting of a modular or
mobile home
with open sides is prohibited from travel unless it is fully enclosed with paneling. The paneling
shall
consist of 0.5 mil plastic sheathing or stronger, backed by a grill work not more than a square four feet by four feet.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-8), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-414. Daylight travel requirements.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load traveling on a
county
highway during daylight hours
shall
comply with the following requirements:
An extra-legal vehicle or load at least 11 feet, but not more than 13 feet in width requires one pilot car in the front.
An extra-legal vehicle or load more than 13 feet, but not more than 15 feet in width requires one pilot car in the front and either one pilot car or one flashing yellow light in the rear.
An extra-legal vehicle or load more than 15 feet in width requires a
special permit
.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-9), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-415. Hours of darkness travel requirements.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load operating or moving during the hours of darkness
shall
comply with the following requirements:
All
lighting
required by C.R.S. §§ 42-4-204—42-4-235 and the Commercial Vehicle Rules, as applicable.
If the extra-legal vehicle or load is not more than 11 feet in width:
A flashing yellow light shall be mounted to the front of the vehicle.
At least two but not more than three flashing yellow lights shall be mounted to the extreme rear.
On all two-lane highways, if the extra-legal vehicle or load is more than 11 feet in width but not more than 13 feet in width, one pilot car escort vehicle shall be in the front.
Any extra legal vehicle or load that is more than 13 feet in width shall only operate in hours of daylight, unless a
special permit
is issued.
If an extra-legal vehicle or load exceeds the
legal limits
for length or has an overhang:
A flashing yellow light shall be mounted to the front of the vehicle.
The
permittee
shall attach at least one but not more than three yellow or red cluster lights to the overhang, as follows:
If the overhang is in the rear, red cluster lights shall be used; and
If the overhang is in the front, yellow cluster lights shall be used.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-10), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-416. Overhead structures and utilities.
The
permittee
shall
be responsible for checking all overhead
structures
and
utilities
on the route to be traveled to ensure that the extra-legal
vehicle
or load has adequate clearance of all overheight restrictions. An extra-legal vehicle or load more than 16 feet in height is prohibited from travel unless a pilot car escort vehicle is positioned in the front.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-11), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-417. Length exceeding maximum; pilot car requirements.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load which exceeds the following
maximum limits
for length must
use
a pilot car escort vehicle:
More than 85 feet when traveling on mountainous two-lane routes, the pilot car escort vehicle must be positioned in the front.
More than 110 feet when traveling on all nonmountainous two-lane highways, the pilot car escort vehicle must be positioned in the front.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-12), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-418. Overhang; pilot car requirements.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load which has an overhang of more than 25 feet must
use
a pilot car escort vehicle. The pilot car escort vehicle
shall
be positioned according to the overhang, as follows:
If the overhang is in the front, the pilot car escort vehicle shall travel in the front.
If the overhang is in the rear, the pilot car escort vehicle shall travel in the rear.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-13), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-419. Highway and bridge restrictions.
All oversize and/or overweight
vehicles
or loads are prohibited from travel at any time in the following situations, unless a
special permit
is issued:
If a bridge or highway has been posted with a maximum weight per vehicle, or is listed in the
bridge weight limit map
or the prohibited overweight route list, no vehicle
may
exceed that maximum weight on that bridge or highway even if the vehicle's weight is otherwise permissible by
transport permit
, issued under this article. (See appendices B and C to Ordinance No. 1997-1, which are on file in the clerk and recorder's office.)
Exceptions to the restrictions in this section may be granted if an
emergency
condition warrants movement and if an agreement can be made between the
permittee
and the
department
.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-14), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
