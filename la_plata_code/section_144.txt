DIVISION 15. LODGING TAX COMMITTEE
FOOTNOTE(S):
Editor's note—
The lodging tax was adopted by referendum on November 3, 1987, which authorized the creation of a lodging tax committee.
Cross reference—
Financial matters, § 2-741 et seq.; taxation, ch. 50; lodging tax, § 50-91 et seq.
State Law reference—
Countywide lodging tax authorized, C.R.S. § 30-11-107.5.
Sec. 2-462. Membership and terms.
I.
The lodging tax committee shall consist of an odd number of not less than three (3) or more than five (5) members appointed by the board of county commissioners from the tourism industry within the municipalities and unincorporated areas from which the tax is collected.
II.
Committee members shall be appointed from the following industries and groups:
A.
The hotel, motel, and lodging industry;
B.
The food, beverage, and restaurant industry;
C.
The ski industry;
D.
Private travel attractions and casinos;
E.
Other outdoor recreation industries;
F.
Tourism-related transportation industries;
G.
The tourism-related retail industry;
H.
The destination marketing industry;
I.
Cultural event and facility groups;
J.
Other tourism-based industries, based on a determination by the board, in its discretion, of the direct correlation between the individual’s industry and tourism.
No individual who owns, is employed or contracted by, represents, manages, sits on the board of, or otherwise exercises any control or oversight of any entity used to advertise and market tourism with funds from the lodger’s tax may be appointed to or remain a member of the committee.
III.
The terms of the members shall be two (2) years.
