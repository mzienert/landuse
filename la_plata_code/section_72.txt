Sec. 2-216. Creation; function.
The
board of county commissioners
does, as a unit of local government within the meaning of C.R.S. § 17-27-101
et seq
., as amended, have the authority to create a Community Corrections Board with the authority to oversee the residential and nonresidential community correction services serving La Plata County, including:
I.
Administer the operations and programs for the community corrections program in La Plata County.
II.
Approve or disapprove the establishment and operation of specific community corrections programs.
III.
Monitor the funds appropriated.
IV.
Receive grants from governmental and private sources and receive court-authorized expense reimbursements related to community corrections programs.
V.
Contract with the State of Colorado, other units of local government, other Community Corrections Boards, any agency of state government or any community corrections program, as necessary, to provide supervision of and services for offenders.
VI.
Establish and enforce standards for the operation of community corrections programs, located in La Plata County, so long as such standards do not conflict with standards established for community corrections programs by the division of criminal justice of the state department of public safety.
VII.
In coordination with state and local agencies, monitor community corrections programs within its jurisdiction and oversee compliance with federal, state and local standards and any contract obligations.
VIII.
Conduct the screening of referrals for placement with any nongovernmental community corrections facilities or programs located within the county.
IX.
Establish conditions and guidelines for the conduct of offenders placed in the community corrections program.
X.
Any other express or incidental authority which may be delegated to the La Plata County Community Corrections Board pursuant to state statute.
(Res. No. 1987-46, § 1, 5-26-1987;
Res. No. 2021-12, § 2-216, 04/20/2021
)
Effective on: 6/1/2021
Sec. 2-217. Administration of funds.
La Plata County does not wish to incur any expense or liability in the operations of the community corrections programs or facilities, all funds allocated for such programs will be obtained, maintained, monitored, administered by and shall be the legal responsibility of the
La Plata County Community Corrections Board
, with any administrative monies received by such entity being utilized to fund an annual financial audit or review, for evaluation of questionable clients, and with any reserve to be used as a contingency fund if local community correction needs exceed available legislative allocations.
(Res. No. 1987-46, § 2, 5-26-1987;
Res. No. 2021-12, § 2-217, 04/20/2021
)
Effective on: 6/1/2021
Sec. 2-218. Composition.
The La Plata County Community Corrections Board shall consist of twelve (12) members and four (4) alternates who shall meet at least quarterly. The membership of such La Plata County Community Corrections Board shall be
comprised of the following representatives (or their designees) appointed by the La Plata County Board of County Commissioners:
To the extent possible, the governing body shall appoint, at its discretion, individuals to serve on the La Plata County Community Corrections Board who represent our ethnic and regional diversity within La Plata County and who are employed by or are members of the following community and/or constituent groups:
I.
Chief Probation Officer of the 6th Judicial District, or their designee
A.
Chief Probation Officer’s named alternate
II.
La Plata County Sheriff, or their designee
A.
La Plata County Sheriff’s named alternate
III.
Public Defender of the 6th Judicial District, or their designee
A.
Public Defender’s named alternate
IV.
The 6th Judicial District Attorney, or their designee
A.
The 6th Judicial District Attorney’s named alternate
V.
A current employee of the State of Colorado Department of Corrections, or their designee
VI.
The Archuleta County Sheriff or their designee
VII.
The 6th Judicial District Chief Judge or their designee
VIII.
A member of the community with a finance or accounting background
IX.
A member of the community who has experience as a mental health/substance abuse treatment provider
X.
A representative from a local business with preference to a business who has employed community corrections clients in the past
XI.
A member of the criminal defense bar in the 6th Judicial District
XII.
A member of the community who represents the victim advocacy community, such as Alternative Horizons, Sexual Assault Services Organization, Rise Above Violence, or a similar organization
XIII.
Up to three members selected from a cross-section of the general citizenry.
A.
While the community corrections program is in a facility on City of Durango-owned land, one of these positions will be offered to the Chief of Police or their Alternate.
If any board member, who may also be represented on the board by a designee or their alternate, cannot attend any meeting of the Community Corrections Board, or a meeting of a Community Corrections subcommittee, may appoint a designee to attend in lieu of the appointed board member or alternate. A designee must be employed by the same agency as the board member who designates them to represent the board member at the meeting.
The La Plata County Community Corrections Board shall meet at least quarterly to attend to La Plata County Community Corrections Board business.
The La Plata County Community Corrections Board shall create a subcommittee, the La Plata County Community Corrections Board Screening Committee (Screening Committee), to meet at least one time per month to review offender cases referred for placement in this program with the authority to accept, defer or reject anyone for placement that is determined to be a risk to the public or the program. The Screening Committee should have at least five, but not more than eight representatives selected from the La Plata County Community Corrections Board.  The recommended make-up of the screening committee should include:
I.
A member of the community who is a mental health/substance abuse treatment provider.
II.
Up to two members selected from a cross-section of general citizenry or the representative from a local business.
III.
A member of the criminal defense bar in the 6
th
Judicial District.
The La Plata County Community Corrections Board shall adopt bylaws no later than December 31, 2021 which shall be subject to acceptance by the La Plata County Board of County
Commissioners, to be reviewed at least every five (5) years thereafter.
The La Plata County Community Corrections Board shall adopt a decision-making tool for use by the Screening Committee no later than December 31, 2021.

Sec. 2-219. Membership; qualifications; application.
The membership of the La Plata County Community Corrections Board shall be by appointment to be made by the appropriate entities involved, as stated in this section following appropriate advertising for such positions. Any interested individuals shall apply in writing, stating in detail the reasons for their applications to serve on the La Plata County Community Corrections Board, and providing pertinent background information in support of their qualifications to serve in that capacity.
(Res. No. 1987-46, § 4, 5-26-1987;
Res. No. 2012-12, § 2-219, 04/20/2021
)
Effective on: 6/1/2021
Sec. 2-220. Terms.
The terms of office for appointees to the La Plata County Communications Corrections Board shall be three (3) years.
