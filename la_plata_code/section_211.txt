Sec. 2-686. Created.
There is hereby established the southwest (region 9) workforce
board
comprised of representatives of the public sector and private sector appointed by, and serving at the pleasure of, the governing bodies of the
counties
of La Plata, Dolores, Montezuma, Archuleta and San Juan, Colorado. The representatives
shall
represent those constituent groups detailed in Executive Order D 000897. At least 50 percent of the membership shall be representatives of the private sector. There should be at least one representative from organized labor and one representative from a community-based organization. Representation of these two entities shall constitute not less than 15 percent of the board membership, and to the extent practicable, it shall be evenly divided.
(Res. No. 1999-8, § 1, 2-22-99)
Effective on: 2/22/1999
Sec. 2-687. Composition.
Every effort should be made to ensure that there is at least one appointee from each of the five
counties
served by the southwest (region 9) workforce
board
.
(Res. No. 1999-8, § 2, 2-22-99)
Effective on: 2/22/1999
Sec. 2-688. Terms.
I.
Initial appointees to the southwest (region 9) workforce
board
and their respective categories are detailed on Annex A hereto (on file in the office of the
county
clerk). Their terms of office
shall
be established at the next meeting of the southwest (region 9) workforce board, and those terms shall be communicated by the southwest (region 9) workforce board chair to the board of county
commissioners
of La Plata, Dolores, Montezuma, Archuleta and San Juan, Colorado as soon as practicable thereafter. Said terms, once established, shall be the terms for the initial appointees to the southwest (region 9) workforce board.
II.
Subsequent appointees shall serve staggered terms of two years each.
(Res. No. 1999-8, §§ 3, 4, 2-22-99)
Effective on: 2/22/1999
Sec. 2-689. Vacancies.
Vacancies
shall
be filled by special appointment made by the governing body of the
county
for which the proposed appointee is a representative; and the appointment shall be to fill the unexpired term of the vacant member's seat.
(Res. No. 1999-8, § 5, 2-22-99)
Effective on: 2/22/1999
Sec. 2-690. Officers; meetings.
I.
The southwest (region 9) workforce
board
shall
elect from its membership a chair and any other officers they deem appropriate for the accomplishment of their tasks. The chair must be a representative of the private business sector.
II.
Meetings of the southwest (region 9) workforce board shall be open to the public and conducted in accordance with the Colorado Sunshine Act. Notices and agendas of public meetings of the southwest (region 9) workforce board shall be posted at the La Plata
County
Administration Building located at 1101 East Second Avenue, Durango, Colorado and at any other locations requested by the governing bodies of the other counties represented on the southwest (region 9) workforce board. The southwest (region 9) workforce board shall meet as often as necessary to fulfill its responsibilities. The quorum needed to transact business must include a majority of the private business sector representatives.
(Res. No. 1999-8, §§ 6, 7, 2-22-99)
Effective on: 10/25/2016
Sec. 2-691. Duties.
The southwest (region 9) workforce
board
shall
have the duties specified by Executive Order D 000897 and shall act in an advisory capacity to the governing bodies of the of the five
counties
served by the southwest (region 9) workforce board. Specifically, the southwest (region 9) workforce board shall prepare a regional workforce plan (as described in Executive Order D 000897) and submit such plan to the governing bodies of the counties of La Plata, Dolores, Montezuma, Archuleta and San Juan, Colorado for consideration and approval.
(Res. No. 1999-8, § 8, 2-22-99)
Effective on: 2/22/1999
