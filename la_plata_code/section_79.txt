Sec. 2-241. Created.
There is established and created an advisory
board
to be known as the library advisory board, which is formed for the purpose of improving and enhancing the
facilities
and services offered to the general public by the Durango Public Library. The library advisory board
shall
be vested with the responsibility of making recommendations with respect to all aspects of the operation and administration of the municipal library. The board shall be advisory and shall perform such functions, duties and responsibilities as are set forth in section 2-243 and such additional duties and responsibilities as
may
be delegated to the board.
(Ord. No. 1987-1, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Effective on: 2/3/1987
Sec. 2-242. Membership; appointment; compensation; removal.
The library advisory
board
shall
consist of five voting members. Three members shall be appointed by the Durango City Council. Two members shall be appointed by the board of
county
commissioners
. Appointments shall be for a three-year term commencing as of the effective date of the appointment. The members are appointed for three-year terms. All appointees shall be eligible for reappointment for additional three-year periods. Board members shall not receive a salary or other compensation for services as a member of the board; provided, however, that necessary traveling and subsistence expenses actually incurred
may
be paid upon prior approval. Members of the library advisory board may be removed only by vote of the appointing authority.
(Ord. No. 1987-1, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Effective on: 2/3/1987
Sec. 2-243. Organization; powers and duties.
I.
The advisory
board
shall
hold an organizational meeting within 30 days after appointment of advisory board members, at which time the members appointed to the board shall meet for purposes of organization and to promptly adopt necessary bylaws for the library advisory board. Such bylaws shall incorporate the terms and provisions of the city "Rules and Procedures for Boards and Commissions, August, 1981." Any and all matters necessary for the proper operation of the library advisory board not covered within this division or within the "Rules and Procedures for Boards and Commissions, August, 1981," shall be covered by the board's bylaws adopted pursuant to such "Rules and Procedures for Boards and Commissions, August, 1981."
II.
The library advisory board shall fulfill the duties and responsibilities to:
A.
Make recommendations on the operation and administration of the Durango Public Library;
B.
Solicit and maintain private funding support for the Durango Public Library;
C.
Make recommendations on policies and procedures for consideration and adoption by the Durango City Council;
D.
Make recommendations to the Durango City Council regarding the library's operations and capital budget;
E.
Review and adopt longterm financial plans for operating and capital needs for the public library
facility
;
F.
Establish, as necessary, advisory subcommittees to assist in the functioning of the Durango Public Library; and
G.
Solicit and maintain support for the public library within the general
community
.
III.
The library advisory board shall also perform such other duties and responsibilities as
may
be designated by the Durango City Council.
(Ord. No. 1987-1, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Effective on: 2/3/1987
Sec. 2-244. Meetings.
The library advisory
board
shall
meet as necessary, but at least bimonthly, to accomplish its duties and responsibilities. The library advisory board shall further hold such public meetings as
may
be required. The library advisory board shall set and review goals and objectives in accordance with the
management
system authorized by the Durango City Council.
(Ord. No. 1987-1, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Effective on: 2/3/1987
Sec. 2-245. Reports.
The library advisory
board
shall
submit an annual written report in conjunction with the
development
of the municipal budget. Such report shall be submitted no later than September 30 of each year and shall detail deficiencies, needs and objectives for the maintenance of the Durango Public Library as an independent learning
facility
for the benefit of the overall
community
. Such report shall also reflect the number of books and periodicals on hand within the public library, the number added by purchase, gift or otherwise during the period covered by the report, the number of books or periodicals which are lost or missing, the number of books loaned out and the general kind and character of such books, with such other statistics, information and suggestions as the library advisory board
may
deem of general interest.
(Ord. No. 1987-1, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Effective on: 2/3/1987
Sec. 2-246. Policy regarding use of library.
The library advisory
board
shall
adopt recommended policies and procedures for consideration by the city council for purposes of ensuring that the library
facility
shall be forever free for the
use
of the residents, always subject to such reasonable rules and regulations as
may
be necessary or appropriate to assure the use of the library facilities for the greatest benefit to the greatest number of people.
(Ord. No. 1987-1, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Effective on: 2/3/1987
Sec. 2-247. Vacancies.
Any appointed member of the library advisory
board
may
resign from such position by providing written notification to the chair of such resignation. If the member was appointed by the
board of county commissioners
, the chair
shall
thereupon notify the board of county commissioners of such resignation, and the board of
county
commissioners
shall thereafter appoint a member to fill such vacancy. The appointee shall serve for the remaining unexpired term of the member whose resignation created the vacancy. Vacancies created by the death of a member of the library advisory board shall be filled in the same manner as vacancies created by resignations of such members.
(Ord. No. 1987-1, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Effective on: 2/3/1987
