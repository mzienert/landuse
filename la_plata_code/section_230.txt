Sec. 2-691.41. Creation of the La Plata County Historic Preservation Review Commission.
I.
There is hereby created the La Plata
County
Historic Preservation Review Commission ("Historic Preservation Review Commission"), to be comprised of seven members appointed by, and serving at the pleasure of, the
board of county commissioners
.
II.
All members must be residents of the county and
shall
have reached the age of 18 years on the effective date of their appointment. Each member shall also have expertise/experience in historic preservation.
III.
The board of county commissioners hereby appoints the individuals listed below, all of whom have hereby agreed to accept such appointment, to be the initial members of the historic preservation review commission, and to initially serve on the commission for the term indicated. At the expiration of each initial term of office below, such individual or such individual's successor shall then serve for a term of three years and until their reappointment or until their respective successor has been appointed. A member
may
be reappointed for successive terms. A member shall hold office until his or her successor has been appointed. Vacancies shall be filled by special appointment made by the board of county commissioners. Such appointment shall be to fill the unexpired term of the vacant member's seat.
IV.
The objectives, duties and purpose of the historic preservation review commission are to:
A.
Review requests for designation of historic landmarks and historic districts; and
B.
Make recommendations to the board of county commissioners with respect to the preservation of cultural, historic, and/or architectural history within the boundaries of the county and any requests for designation of historic landmarks and historic districts.
V.
Three members shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business by the historic preservation review commission.
Action
of the historic preservation review commission shall be by majority vote of those voting. A tie vote shall not constitute affirmative action by the historic preservation review commission. The historic preservation review commission may call upon the county attorney for such legal services as it may require.
VI.
The historic preservation commission members shall elect from their membership a chair, vice-chair, and any other officers they deem appropriate for the accomplishment of their task. Meetings of the historic preservation commission shall be open to the public and conducted in accordance with the Colorado open meetings law (C.R.S. 24-6-402). Notices and agendas of public meetings of the historic preservation commission shall be posted at the County Administration Building located at 1101 East Second Avenue, Durango, Colorado. The historic preservation commission shall meet as often as necessary to fulfill its responsibilities.
VII.
The historic preservation commission shall remain in existence until the board of county commissioners determines that its purpose and objectives have been fulfilled.
(Res. No. 2004-33, § 1, 11-15-2004; Res. No. 2010-09, §§ 1, 2, 2-9-2010)

Sec. 2-691.42. Designation of historic landmark or historic district.
I.
The procedure for requesting historic landmark or historic district designation, along with the forms for such designation,
shall
be as determined by the
county
planning
department
subject to recommendation by the county historic preservation review commission and approval by the county
board
of
commissioners
.
II.
Any
person
or entity seeking historic landmark or historic district designation for any land,
facility
or
structure
within the boundaries of unincorporated county must submit the request on the required form, along with all required documentation, to the county planning department who shall then, upon completion of their review, forward the request to the county historic preservation review board for a recommendation.
III.
Upon receipt of a recommendation from the county historic preservation review board, the
board of county commissioners
shall, within 30 days at a regularly scheduled meeting, make a determination regarding the request for historic designation. Should such determination be in favor of historic designation, the board of county commissioners shall officially proclaim such designation by county resolution.
IV.
The county board of county commissioners shall establish the La Plata County Historic Register, which shall be maintained in the office of the county clerk and recorder, and the county planning department, in files titled, "La Plata County Historic Register."
(Res. No. 2004-33, § 1, 11-15-2004; Res. No. 2008-30, § 1, 11-12-2008)
Effective on: 11/12/2008
