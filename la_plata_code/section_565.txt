Sec. 42-91. Definitions.
As used herein, the following terms or phrases
shall
mean:
County Road
means a
ll public highways on the county road system as shown on the map submitted to the state department of transportation pursuant to C.R.S. § 43-2-120.
(
Ord. No. 2021-04, §  06/08/2021
)
Effective on: 7/11/2021
Adverse weather conditions
means a weather condition or occurrence, including normal seasonal weather conditions such as snow, that renders the maintenance of a road cost-prohibitive or vehicular access to the road infeasible.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Arterial road
means a road that provides for higher traffic volumes over medium to long distances and is used for inter-regional, inter-county and intra-county travel needs or serves as a main collector.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Local road
means a farm to market road or road that provides for lower to medium traffic volumes and distributes traffic to arterial roads.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Maintained on a continual basis
means a road will be considered to be maintained on a continual basis if it meets the following criteria:
It does not include any continuous significant obstructions including but not limited to structures, ditches, waterways, locked gates, fences, trees, rocks, sand and dirt; and
It is substantially clear of vegetation; and
It is annually bladed, graded or otherwise maintained in a manner determined by the county road supervisor or other statutory or governmental entity to be appropriate for the given road classification and to maintain a condition of reasonable passability for ordinary motor vehicles.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Primary road
means a road that the board of county commissioners determines to have great general importance to the county and traveling public and is either classified as an arterial road (administrative classification 1) or local road (administrative classification 2) and for which the county or another statutory entity or governmental body within the county receives HUTF.
Road repair or
maintenance agreement
means an agreement or intergovernmental agreement between the county and one or more third-parties including other governmental or statutory entities wherein such third-parties agree to make financial or in-kind contributions to the repair of maintenance of a county road. Such agreements with private third parties shall, subject to the discretion of the county engineer, county attorney and the board, substantially conform to the road repair and maintenance agreement attached to Res. No. 2002-13 as exhibit A, on file at the county clerk's office.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Secondary road
means a road that the board of county commissioners determines does not have great general importance to the traveling public and/or roads for which the county does not receive HUTF. Such roads may consist of public highways that are open to the public but not maintained by the county. For instance, such roads may be maintained by other federal, city or state agencies such as the Forest Service, Bureau of Indian Affairs, the City of Durango or Bureau of Land Management. A secondary road may also be a public highway that is or is not open to the public for all permissible uses.
(Res. No. 2002-13, § 1, 3-11-2002)
Effective on: 3/11/2002

Primary roads
within the
county
's road system

meet the following criteria:
Be legally open to public travel by ordinary motor

Sec. 42-93. Maintenance level for secondary roads.
The level of maintenance of a
secondary road
shall
be the historic level of maintenance of such road as determined on a case-by-case basis by the road supervisor. Upon agreement by the
board
, the quality of a secondary road

be improved or maintained as provided in a road repair or maintenance agreement by and between the
county
and a third party.

The
board of county commissioners
shall
cause a road maintenance map to be prepared showing each road in the
county
's primary and secondary system as of the date of the map and the corresponding level of maintenance of each road. At least once a year, the
board
shall cause notice of its intent to consider, revise and adopt the road maintenance map to be given, which notice shall specify the time and place at which all interested
persons
will be heard. Such notice of intention shall be published once a week for at least two successive weeks preceding the date of such hearing in a newspaper of general circulation in the county. After such hearing, the board of county commissioners shall adopt the road maintenance map, with any changes or revisions deemed by it to be advisable. Such map shall establish the maintenance level of each road for the year. Such map shall not constitute the official map of the road system of the county.
Effective on: 2/21/2023
Sec. 42-95. Long-term restrictions on access.
The Board of County Commissioners may adopt resolutions restricting the long-term access of any county road, pursuant to this section, as amended from time to time, as determined necessary to preserve public health, safety and welfare of residents and travelling public. The
board
's decision as to whether to restrict access to a  county road, including but not limited to a closure of the same, for a period in excess of ninety days
shall
be determined as follows:
I.
The
county
engineer
shall make a recommendation to the board as to whether the proposed restriction is necessary to protect public health, safety and welfare, to maintain smooth traffic flow, to maintain highway
right-of-way
drainage or to protect the functional level of the county road.
II.
The board shall hold at least one public hearing to consider and take testimony as to the proposed restriction(s).
III.
The necessity of the restriction(s) shall be reviewed on an annual basis.
IV.
When appropriate, the county engineer
may
recommend or the board may require that a road repair and maintenance agreement be entered into to maintain a minimal functional level of the road or to prevent further deterioration.
V.
The board shall make a formal finding that the restrictions are necessary and subject to the board's reasonable police powers.
Failure to adhere to long-term restrictions on access to a county road shall be considered a traffic infraction, as set forth in Article V of Chapter 54 of the Code.
(Res. No. 2002-13, § 5, 3-11-2002;
Ord. No. 2021-04, §  06/08/2021
)
Effective on: 7/11/2021
