Sec. 42-201. Permit requirements for extra-legal vehicle or load.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load identified in C.R.S. § 42-4-510(9)
shall
be exempt from the requirement to obtain a
permit
.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-1), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-202. Legal except height.
Vehicles
or loads that are within all
legal limits
, except height, are exempt from the
lighting
requirements of section 42-415.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-2), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-203. Height pole exempt from permit.
A pilot car escort
vehicle
operating with a height pole which exceeds the legal limit for height
shall
be exempt from the requirement to obtain a
permit
for the pole.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-3), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-204. Emergency moves.
Emergency
moves of an extra-legal
vehicle
or load are exempt from the requirement to obtain a
permit
to move an endangered extra-legal vehicle or load out of the danger area to a temporary or new permanent location, subject to the
bridge weight limit map
restrictions. However, an extra-legal vehicle or load is required to obtain a permit to move from the temporary safe location to a new or original permanent location. All emergency moves
shall
comply with the following:
If practicable, verbal authorization shall be obtained prior to the move, as follows:
If the emergency occurs during business hours the
department
must be contacted; and
If the emergency occurs at night, on weekends or holidays, the
county
sheriff must be contacted.
This article, unless otherwise authorized by the department or sheriff.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-4), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-205. Commercial snow removal vehicles.
Commercial snow removal
vehicles
that are within all
legal limits
except width
shall
be exempt from the travel time restrictions of section 42-406, the sign requirements of section 42-411, and the pilot car escort vehicle requirements of section 42-441, subject to the following conditions:
The vehicle must not exceed 12 feet in width; and
The snow removal blade must be raised and turned parallel to the highway as much as possible, and when raised and turned, shall not exceed 12 feet in width with respect to the highway, at all times when the vehicle is moving on a
county
highway but not plowing snow, so that the width of the blade to oncoming traffic is minimized.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-5), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-206. Legal except weight.
A
vehicle
or load which is within all
legal limits
except weight
shall
be exempt from the sign requirements of section
42-411.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-6), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-207. Longer vehicle combinations.
Longer
vehicle
combinations
shall
be exempt from the following requirements of this article:
Sections 42-213 and 42-245, regarding documents required to be in the permitted vehicle.
Section 42-408, regarding divisible loads.
Section 42-411, regarding sign requirements.
Section 42-417, regarding length restrictions.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-7), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-208. Exempt divisible loads.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load which does not exceed the
maximum limits
for weight and which detaches and carries
equipment
essential to its operation, i.e., dozer, ripper, counterweights, etc.,
shall
be exempt from section 42-408 regarding divisible loads.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-8), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-209. Crane boom sections.
Crane boom sections transported side by side as items essential to the operation of the crane
shall
be exempt from section 42-408 regarding divisible loads, provided that such sections do not exceed an overall width of 11 feet, six inches, and provided that the trailer
unit
carrying the crane boom sections travels on the highways with and directly behind the crane. Notwithstanding section 42-412, a distance of not less than 100 feet nor more than 500 feet shall be maintained between the crane and the trailer unit.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-9), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-210. Precast concrete panels.
A precast concrete panel being transported on an A frame apparatus mounted on a trailer
shall
be exempt from the requirements of section 42-408 regarding divisible loads, and
may
utilize up to three additional concrete panels to balance the load.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-10), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-211. Towing wreckers.
Towing wreckers
shall
be exempt from the requirement to obtain a
permit
only when towing a disabled extra-legal
vehicle
or load from the highway to the nearest suitable location. However, a towing wrecker must obtain a permit when towing the disabled extra-legal vehicle or load beyond the nearest suitable location, or from the nearest suitable location to another location.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-11), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-212. Vehicle crossings.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load entering
county
highway
right-of-way
for the sole purpose of immediately crossing the county highway, on a one-time basis, is exempt from the requirement to obtain a
permit
if the extra-legal vehicle or load does not exceed the annual permit specifications listed in section
42-309.
The
permittee
of an extra-legal vehicle or load which exceeds the annual permit specifications of section 42-309
shall
contact the
department
prior to crossing the county highway to determine if a permit will be required. In determining whether a permit will be required, the department shall consider the number of proposed crossings, seasonal and weather conditions, time of crossings, protection of efficient movement of traffic, protection of the highway from undue damage to the road, width of the county right-of-way involved and any other factors relevant to the specific right-of-way.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(7-12), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-213. Special mobile equipment exemption (SMEE).
Special
mobile
equipment
exemptions
shall
be recognized in the
county
, provided that proof of an exemption certificate issued by the state is made for the specific
vehicle
. All state requirements for moving special mobile equipment shall be applicable to any and all moves within the county.
A copy of the exemption certificate shall be carried in the vehicle or combination of vehicles to which it refers and shall be open to inspection by any police officer or authorized
agent
of any authority granting such exemption letter, or by the
department
. A copy of the exemption certificate shall be provided to the
permit officer
.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(8-1), (8-2), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Sec. 42-214. Military and emergency vehicles.
Military and
emergency
vehicles
are exempt from this article's provisions.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(2-11), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
Secs. 42-215—42-235. Reserved.
Effective on: 9/8/1997
