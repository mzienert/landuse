DIVISION 28. HISTORIC PRESERVATION REVIEW COMMISSION
FOOTNOTE(S):
Editor's note—
Res. No. 2004-33, § 1, adopted November 15, 2004, did not specify manner of inclusion; hence, inclusion as division 28, §§ 2-691.41 and 2-691.42 is at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 2-691.42. Designation of historic landmark or historic district.
I.
The procedure for requesting historic landmark or historic district designation, along with the forms for such designation,
shall
be as determined by the
county
planning
department
subject to recommendation by the county historic preservation review commission and approval by the county
board
of
commissioners
.
II.
Any
person
or entity seeking historic landmark or historic district designation for any land,
facility
or
structure
within the boundaries of unincorporated county must submit the request on the required form, along with all required documentation, to the county planning department who shall then, upon completion of their review, forward the request to the county historic preservation review board for a recommendation.
III.
Upon receipt of a recommendation from the county historic preservation review board, the
board of county commissioners
shall, within 30 days at a regularly scheduled meeting, make a determination regarding the request for historic designation. Should such determination be in favor of historic designation, the board of county commissioners shall officially proclaim such designation by county resolution.
IV.
The county board of county commissioners shall establish the La Plata County Historic Register, which shall be maintained in the office of the county clerk and recorder, and the county planning department, in files titled, "La Plata County Historic Register."
(Res. No. 2004-33, § 1, 11-15-2004; Res. No. 2008-30, § 1, 11-12-2008)
Effective on: 11/12/2008
