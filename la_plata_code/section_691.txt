DIVISION 6. TRANSPORT PERMIT OPERATING REQUIREMENTS
Sec. 42-415. Hours of darkness travel requirements.
An extra-legal
vehicle
or load operating or moving during the hours of darkness
shall
comply with the following requirements:
All
lighting
required by C.R.S. §§ 42-4-204—42-4-235 and the Commercial Vehicle Rules, as applicable.
If the extra-legal vehicle or load is not more than 11 feet in width:
A flashing yellow light shall be mounted to the front of the vehicle.
At least two but not more than three flashing yellow lights shall be mounted to the extreme rear.
On all two-lane highways, if the extra-legal vehicle or load is more than 11 feet in width but not more than 13 feet in width, one pilot car escort vehicle shall be in the front.
Any extra legal vehicle or load that is more than 13 feet in width shall only operate in hours of daylight, unless a
special permit
is issued.
If an extra-legal vehicle or load exceeds the
legal limits
for length or has an overhang:
A flashing yellow light shall be mounted to the front of the vehicle.
The
permittee
shall attach at least one but not more than three yellow or red cluster lights to the overhang, as follows:
If the overhang is in the rear, red cluster lights shall be used; and
If the overhang is in the front, yellow cluster lights shall be used.
(Ord. No. 1997-1, § 3(4-10), 9-8-1997)
Effective on: 9/8/1997
