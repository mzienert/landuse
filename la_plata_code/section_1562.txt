Sec. 98-5. Permits required.
A
permit
shall
be required to install a
directional sign
and
business plaque
in the
county
road
right-of-way
or within 30 feet from a county road shoulder. A permit shall be valid for one year, expiring on
May
1 of each year.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
Sec. 98-6. Directional sign permit process.
A
person
or entity wishing to obtain a
directional sign
and
business plaque
permit
must submit an application and fee to the
administrator
. The fee
shall
be set by the administrator and shall cover the cost of administrating this article. An application
may
be obtained from the
county
public works
department
. The administrator shall review the application and determine whether the application is complete or whether additional information is required to process the application. If additional information is required, the administrator shall inform the
applicant
in writing. A completed application shall be reviewed by the administrator for compliance with the eligibility and
site
location requirements of this article. The administrator shall deny an application that fails to meet the eligibility and site location requirements of this article. If an application is denied, the administrator shall provide the applicant with a written notice of the denial and the basis for such denial.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
Sec. 98-7. Eligibility.
A
person
or entity is eligible to place a
directional sign
and
business plaque
in the
county
road
right-of-way
if the following conditions are met:
The person or entity derives the major portion of its income or visitors from motorists not residing in the immediate area (25 miles of the business).
The business is located at least 300 driving feet and less than 15 driving miles of the intersection where the directional sign is proposed to be installed.
The business is open to the general public during regular and reasonable business hours.
The business is open at least 20 hours per week and seasonal operations are in operation at least eight consecutive weeks per year.
There is available space for the directional sign at the requested intersection, as determined by the public works
director
utilizing standard engineering practices, and the sign does not interfere with driver sight of existing or planned official traffic
control
devices.
If the access to the business is on a major collector or arterial, the
facility
is not recognizable to a motorist who is within 300 feet of the access
driveway
or intersection leading to the business, service or activity.
If, in the public works director's discretion, a straight ahead movement is not the most logical and prevalent movement for the traveling public.
Prior to directional sign installation, additional
trailblazer
signs that are required to assist motorists locate the business, as determined by the county, are installed.
Prior to directional sign installation, the business obtains proper county land
use
approval or is a legal
nonconforming use
.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011, Res. No. 2017-11)
Effective on: 3/23/2017
Sec. 98-8. Site location.
Directional signs
shall
not create an unsafe driving condition and shall not be in conflict with other traffic
control
devices.
Directional signs,
business plaques
and
trailblazers
shall be within the
county
right-of-way
.
Directional signs shall be located as to take advantage of natural terrain and have the least impact on the scenic environment.
Location placement, position, height, and lateral clearance of the directional signs and the business plaques thereon shall be in a manner consistent with these regulations and in accordance with the
MUTCD
.
Business plaques shall only be located on directional signs.
A maximum of two directional signs, with each accommodating no more than four business plaques shall be allowed for each approach to an intersection.
Directional signs shall be at least 200 feet from: another directional sign, other official traffic control devices, and from the intersection where the turn to exit the conventional road is necessary to get to the business.
When approaching an intersection where two directional signs are located, the order of the signs shall be signs for the left direction, signs for the right direction, signs for straight ahead.
When approved business plaques for multiple directions are attached to one directional sign, the top businesses shall be for the left direction, the bottom businesses shall be for the right direction.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
Sec. 98-9. Design.
All permitted
directional signs
and
business plaques
shall
be designed and installed in accordance with the following requirements:
Directional sign.
A directional sign shall consist of an assembly of one or two posts and a maximum of four authorized business plaques, depending upon engineering design specifications for the location.
The erection and maintenance of directional signs shall be the responsibility of the
county
or its designee.
All directional signs shall be provided and constructed in accordance with all
MUTCD
state and local requirements.
Business plaques.
Business plaques shall contain a name of the business and shall be attached to the directional sign.
Business plaques shall be rectangular in shape and have a blue reflective background with a white reflective legend and border.
The plaques shall be 48 inches wide and 18 inches high.
Each plaque shall contain no more than two lines of legend and
may
include one symbol.
The legend shall be white on a blue background. Content to the legend shall be limited to identification of the business, service, or activity, the mileage to the business, and the directional arrow. Legends shall not contain promotional advertising and all legends are subject to approval by the county or its designee.
The legend may contain nationally approved symbols for services. Standard general service symbols, and white recreational and cultural interest area symbols, as displayed in the MUTCD, may be used to indicate a general class of business.
Logos may be contained on plaques. The logo shall be reproduced in the colors and general shape consistent with customary
use
and any integral legend shall be in proportionate size. No logos shall resemble official
control
devices.
The plaque shall include a separate directional arrow and distance to the business to the nearest one-quarter mile.
The erection and maintenance of business plaques shall be the responsibility of the county or its designee.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011; Res. No. 2011-22, § 1, 7-19-2011)
Effective on: 7/19/2011
Sec. 98-10. Renewals.
Applications for renewal of
directional sign
and
business plaque
permits
shall
be made by the
applicant
and received by the
administrator
on or before April 1 of each year, and shall be accompanied by the annual fee. The
county
may
deny an application for a renewal if, in the sole discretion of the administrator, the permitting and placement of a directional sign at a certain location would endanger the health, safety or welfare of the public or would be inconsistent with the standard engineering practices, or the administrator finds that there is an existing
violation
of the eligibility requirements of this article. If a renewal application is denied, the administrator shall provide the applicant with written notice of the denial, which explains the basis, and shall return the annual fee that was submitted with the renewal application. If a permit renewal is not received or denied, the business plaque shall be removed.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
Sec. 98-11. Revocation and removal.
Failure to comply with standards. The
administrator
may
revoke a
directional sign
and
business plaque
permit
and remove the sign if he or she determines that the sign was altered so that it no longer meets the design or location requirements or if the administrator finds that the
permittee
no longer meets the eligibility requirements found in this chapter. The administrator
shall
provide written notice to the permittee if the permit is revoked.
Removal for
county
purposes. The county may remove any permitted directional sign and business plaque in the event the location of such sign is needed for
roadway
activities including, but not limited to,
construction
, and reconstruction or maintenance. If the county removes the directional sign and business plaque, it shall attempt to work with the permittee to find a substitute location. If a substitute location cannot be found, the permittee shall be entitled to an appropriate pro-rata rebate of that part of paid fees applicable to the unused remainder of the term.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
Sec. 98-12. Replacement procedures.
The
administrator
shall
perform any necessary yearly maintenance of a
directional sign
and
business plaque
. This includes repair or replacement of damaged or destroyed signs due to vehicular impact or work done within the
right-of-way
. If a directional sign and business plaque needs to be replaced or substantially repaired for other causes, such as theft or vandalism, the administrator shall replace or repair the sign for the first occurrence only. Costs for subsequent repair or replacement will be the responsibility of the
permittee
.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
Sec. 98-13. Program administration.
In accordance with other local regulations, the
county
may
negotiate and contract with an independent contractor to implement and administer the regulations described in this article.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
Sec. 98-14. Limited rights.
Granting of the
directional sign
and
business plaque
permit
conveys no right, title or interest to the
permittees
in the highway rights-of-way or in the directional sign and business plaque other than permission to
use
and occupy such location for directional sign purposes subject to these regulations. Directional signs and plaques
shall
remain the exclusive property of the
county
.
(Res. No. 2011-16, § 1(Exh. A), 6-14-2011)
Effective on: 6/14/2011
