Sec. 2-566. Created; membership; terms.
I.
That there is hereby created the living with
wildlife
advisory
board
(the board), formerly known as the predatory animal
control
committee, to be comprised of six members, including one representative of the Southern Ute Indian Tribe, and an indefinite number of alternates. All members and alternates must be residents of the
county
, and
shall
be appointed by the
board of county commissioners
. All members and alternates shall serve at the pleasure of the board of county commissioners. All appointees shall have reached the age of 18 on the effective date of their appointment.
II.
Appointees shall serve staggered terms of three years each.
III.
Members of the board shall serve until their successors are appointed.
(Res. No. 2002-6, §§ 1—3, 1-28-2002; Res. No. 2007-10, § 2, 2-13-2007; Res. No. 2007-18, § 1, 4-10-2007)
Effective on: 4/10/2007
Sec. 2-567. Vacancies.
Vacancies
shall
be filled by special appointment made by the
board
of
county
commissioners
of the
county
. Such appointment shall be to fill the unexpired term of the vacant member's seat.
(Res. No. 2002-6, § 4, 1-28-2002)
Effective on: 1/28/2002
Sec. 2-568. Officers; meetings; quorum.
The
board
members
shall
elect from their membership a chair, vice-chair, and any other officers they deem appropriate for the accomplishment of their task. Meetings of the board shall be open to the public and conducted in accordance with the Colorado open meetings law (C.R.S. § 24-6-402). Notices and agendas of public meetings of the board shall be posted at the La Plata
County
Administration Building located at 1101 East Second Avenue, Durango, Colorado. The board shall meet as often as necessary to fulfill its responsibilities.
The presence of a majority of the board shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Such board shall have the duties as provided in this division and shall act in an advisory capacity to the board of county
commissioners
.
(Res. No. 2002-6, §§ 5, 6, 1-28-2002; Res. No. 2007-10, § 2, 2-13-2007)
Effective on: 10/25/2016
Sec. 2-569. Duties.
The duties of the
board
shall
be:
I.
Work collaboratively with the United States
Department
of
Agriculture
, Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service,
wildlife
services officer, and other agencies as appropriate, to develop and implement programs to reduce animal damage within the
county
;
II.
Develop and coordinate educational programs to assist
community
members in living with wildlife on their property safely and appropriately; and
III.
Review any specific matters submitted to it for consideration and perform such other functions as prescribed by the
board of county commissioners
.
The board
may
create and appoint members to any subcommittees it deems appropriate to accomplish these duties; however, any
actions
, plans or programs developed by any subcommittee must be reviewed and approved by the committee prior to implementation. In
addition
, any policy recommendations of any subcommittee to the board of county commissioners must also be reviewed and approved by the committee prior to the presentation to, and/or formal consideration by the board of county commissioners.
(Res. No. 2002-6, § 7, 2-11-2002; Res. No. 2007-10, § 2, 2-13-2007)
Effective on: 2/13/2007
Sec. 2-570. Expenditure of funds related to wildlife education.
The
board
shall
coordinate with the assistant to the
county
manager with respect to any proposed expenditure of funds related to
wildlife
education programs and/or materials.
(Res. No. 2002-6, § 8, 2-11-2002; Res. No. 2007-10, § 2, 2-13-2007)
Effective on: 2/13/2007
