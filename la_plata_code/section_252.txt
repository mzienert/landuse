Sec. 2-691.111. Creation; composition; terms.
I.
That there is hereby created the placement alternatives commission (hereinafter referred to as the "core services - therapeutic and life skills commission").
II.
The core services - therapeutic and life skills commission
shall
be comprised, where possible, of the following:
A.
A physician or other licensed health professional;
B.
An attorney;
C.
Representatives from local law enforcement;
D.
Representatives from the court and probation
department
;
E.
Representatives from the
county
department of human services;
F.
Representatives from a local mental health clinic;
G.
Representatives from the local public health department;
H.
Representatives of a local school district specializing in special education;
I.
Representatives of a local
community
centered
board
;
J.
Representatives of a local
residential
child care
facility
;
K.
Representatives of a private not for profit agency providing nonresidential services for children and
families
;
L.
Representatives specializing in occupational training or employment programs;
M.
A foster parent;
N.
One or more representatives of the lay community.
III.
At least six members of the core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall represent the private sector.
IV.
The core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall consist of 12 members appointed by, and serving at the pleasure of, the
board of county commissioners
. The initial 12 members of the core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall consist of 12 qualified members of the Sixth Judicial District SB-94 Juvenile Planning Committee, which has served as the interim placement alternatives commission. Members and their initial terms are noted in exhibit A attached to Res. No. 2009-41.
V.
Appointees shall serve staggered terms of three years each, with terms ending January 31st of any given year.
VI.
All members must be residents of or work in the county.
(Res. No. 2009-41, §§ 1—5, 8, 12-8-2009; Res. No. 2012-28, § 1, 6-26-2012)
Effective on: 6/26/2012
Sec. 2-691.112. Objectives; duties; purpose.
I.
The objectives, duties and purpose of the core services - therapeutic and life skills commission are to assure access to alternatives to out-of-home placements for
families
with children at imminent risk of out-of-home placements. The core services - therapeutic and life skills commission
shall
annually prepare a plan for the provision of services. The primary goals of the plan shall be to prevent imminent placement of children out of the home and to re-unite children who have been placed out of the home with their families. The plan shall be prepared using all available sources of information in the
community
, including public hearings. The plan shall specify the nature of the expenditures to be made and shall identify the services which are intended to prevent or minimize placement out of the home and to what extent. The plan shall contain, whenever practicable, a vocational component to provide assistance to older children concerning a transition into the work force upon completion of school. Upon approval of the plan by the
county
commissioners
, the county shall submit the plan to the
department
of human services.
II.
The core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall prepare multi-year plans for the commission's services. The multi-year plans
may
be amended annually for budgetary or programmatic changes that are necessary to enhance service delivery or as otherwise deemed necessary to accomplish the goals of the plan, which reasons shall be set forth in state
board
rules. Counties shall submit the multi-year plans for approval by the state board of human services.
III.
The core services - therapeutic and life skills commission may make recommendations to the board concerning positions on issues of importance to the county, but may not take positions itself.
(Res. No. 2009-41, §§ 6, 7, 11, 12-8-2009)
Effective on: 12/8/2009
Sec. 2-691.113. Election of officers; meetings; quorum.
I.
The core services - therapeutic and life skills commission members
shall
elect from their membership a chair, vice-chair, and any other officers they deem appropriate for the accomplishment of their task. Meetings of the core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall be open to the public and conducted in accordance with the Colorado Open Meetings Law (C.R.S. § 24-6-402). Notices and agendas of public meetings of the core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall be posted at the La Plata
County
Administration Building located at 1101 East Second Avenue, Durango, Colorado. The core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall meet as often as necessary to fulfill its responsibilities.
II.
The presence of a majority of the core services - therapeutic and life skills commission shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Such commission shall have the powers and duties as provided in C.R.S. § 19-1-116 and shall act in an advisory capacity to the
board
of county
commissioners
pursuant to C.R.S. § 19-1-116.
(Res. No. 2009-41, §§ 9, 10, 12-8-2009)
Effective on: 10/25/2016
Sec. 2-691.114. Compensation.
Members of the core services - therapeutic and life skills commission
shall
serve without compensation.
(Res. No. 2009-41, § 12, 12-8-2009)
Effective on: 12/8/2009
