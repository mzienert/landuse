Sec. 42-31. Permit required.
Any event, such as a bicycle race, ride, foot race, walk or any other event that utilizes a highway or
county
road within the unincorporated portions of the county and
may
require changing, restricting or adapting the normal traffic
use
of such a road is required to obtain a special event
permit
.
(Res. No. 2013-09, § 1(Exh. A), 2-5-2013)
Effective on: 2/5/2013
Sec. 42-32. Planning department authorized to accept applications.
The planning
department
is authorized to accept applications and attendant documentation for special events
permits
, and is responsible for soliciting comment regarding the permit from the
county
sheriff's office, the county office of
emergency
management
, and the department of public works. Whenever any portion of an event proposes the use of a highway or county road within the exterior boundaries of the Southern Ute Indian Reservation, the planning department shall also provide written notice of the permit application to, and solicit comment from, the Southern Ute Indian Tribe.
Res. No.
2013-09
, § 1, 02/05/2013; Res. No.
2024-25
, §  07/23/2024
Effective on: 7/23/2024
Sec. 42-33. Application review and consideration.
Upon acceptance of an application and receipt of comments, the planning
department
shall
provide the application materials to the public works
director
. The public works director is authorized to approve special events
permit
applications and partially or completely close or restrict traffic for an event in accordance with C.R.S. § 24-33.5-226, as amended. The director shall require adequate proof of insurance liability coverage for the event, an operational and traffic
control
plan, an
emergency
management plan
, a parking plan and a communication plan for interface with law enforcement personnel. The director
may
include appropriate conditions as part of the approval to protect the health, safety and welfare of the public.
(Res. No. 2013-09, § 1(Exh. A), 2-5-2013)
Effective on: 2/5/2013
Sec. 42-34. Permit revocation.
A
permit
may
be revoked due to unforeseen conditions, including but not limited to, fires,
floods
, or other unexpected circumstances.
(Res. No. 2013-09, § 1(Exh. A), 2-5-2013)
Effective on: 2/5/2013
