Sec. 2-166. Creation; composition.
I.
There is hereby created the Long-term Finance Committee ("LTFC"), which shall be comprised of seven (7) regular members and two (2) alternate members, appointed by, and serving at the pleasure of, the Board of County Commissioners.
II.
The
director
of finance
shall
be an ex-officio member of the long-term finance committee. Other ad hoc ex-officio members
may
be appointed by the
board
of
county
commissioners
.
(Res. No. 2009-07, §§ 1, 1-27-2009; Res. No. 2020-08 01-28-2020)
Effective on: 1/27/2009
Sec. 2-167. Terms; vacancies; qualifications of members.
Appointees
shall
serve staggered terms of three years each. Members of the LTFC shall serve until their successors are appointed.
Vacancies shall be filled by special appointment made by the
board
of
county
commissioners
. Such appointment shall be to fill the unexpired term of the vacant member's seat.
All members must be residents of the
county
.
The LTFC members shall be impartial observers of the county's long term financial interests. The members of the committee, excluding ex-officio members, shall not hold any other office in county government or be an employee of the county.
(Res. No. 2009-07, §§ 2—6, 1-27-2009)
Effective on: 1/27/2009
Sec. 2-168. Election of officers; meetings; quorum.
The LTFC members
shall
elect from their membership a chair, vice-chair, and any other officers they deem appropriate for the accomplishment of their task. Meetings of the LTFC shall be open to the public and conducted in accordance with the Colorado open meetings law (C.R.S. § 24-6-402). Notices and agendas of public meetings of the LTFC shall be posted at the La Plata
County
Administration Building located at 1101 East Second Avenue, Durango, Colorado. The LTFC shall meet as often as necessary to fulfill its responsibilities.
The presence of a majority of the LTFC shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.
(Res. No. 2009-07, §§ 7, 8, 1-27-2009)
Effective on: 10/25/2016
Sec. 2-169. Purpose; duties; functions; compensation.
The purpose of the LTFC is to act solely in an advisory capacity to the
board of county commissioners
with respect to long-term financial planning.
Duties of the LTFC are to:
Review revenue and expenditure trends with a view to identifying potential financial threats and opportunities;
Make recommendations to maintain a structurally balanced budget and preserve the
county
's fiscal soundness.
Review capital
improvement
programs and the recommendations for funding those improvements.
The LTFC
may
make recommendations to the
board
concerning positions on issues of importance to the county, but may not take positions itself.
LTFC members
shall
serve without compensation.
The LTFC shall remain in existence until the board of county
commissioners
determines that its purpose and objectives have been fulfilled.
(Res. No. 2009-07, §§ 9—13, 1-27-2009)
Effective on: 1/27/2009
