Sec. 42-91. Definitions.
As used herein, the following terms or phrases
shall
mean:
County Road
means a
ll public highways on the county road system as shown on the map submitted to the state department of transportation pursuant to C.R.S. § 43-2-120.
(
Ord. No. 2021-04, §  06/08/2021
)
Effective on: 7/11/2021
Adverse weather conditions
means a weather condition or occurrence, including normal seasonal weather conditions such as snow, that renders the maintenance of a road cost-prohibitive or vehicular access to the road infeasible.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Arterial road
means a road that provides for higher traffic volumes over medium to long distances and is used for inter-regional, inter-county and intra-county travel needs or serves as a main collector.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Local road
means a farm to market road or road that provides for lower to medium traffic volumes and distributes traffic to arterial roads.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Maintained on a continual basis
means a road will be considered to be maintained on a continual basis if it meets the following criteria:
It does not include any continuous significant obstructions including but not limited to structures, ditches, waterways, locked gates, fences, trees, rocks, sand and dirt; and
It is substantially clear of vegetation; and
It is annually bladed, graded or otherwise maintained in a manner determined by the county road supervisor or other statutory or governmental entity to be appropriate for the given road classification and to maintain a condition of reasonable passability for ordinary motor vehicles.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Primary road
means a road that the board of county commissioners determines to have great general importance to the county and traveling public and is either classified as an arterial road (administrative classification 1) or local road (administrative classification 2) and for which the county or another statutory entity or governmental body within the county receives HUTF.
Road repair or
maintenance agreement
means an agreement or intergovernmental agreement between the county and one or more third-parties including other governmental or statutory entities wherein such third-parties agree to make financial or in-kind contributions to the repair of maintenance of a county road. Such agreements with private third parties shall, subject to the discretion of the county engineer, county attorney and the board, substantially conform to the road repair and maintenance agreement attached to Res. No. 2002-13 as exhibit A, on file at the county clerk's office.
Effective on: 3/11/2002
Secondary road
means a road that the board of county commissioners determines does not have great general importance to the traveling public and/or roads for which the county does not receive HUTF. Such roads may consist of public highways that are open to the public but not maintained by the county. For instance, such roads may be maintained by other federal, city or state agencies such as the Forest Service, Bureau of Indian Affairs, the City of Durango or Bureau of Land Management. A secondary road may also be a public highway that is or is not open to the public for all permissible uses.
(Res. No. 2002-13, § 1, 3-11-2002)
Effective on: 3/11/2002
