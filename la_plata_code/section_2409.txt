Geologic Hazard
means a geologic phenomenon which is so adverse to past, current, or foreseeable construction or land use as to constitute a significant hazard to public health and safety, or to property. The term includes but is not limited to:
1. Avalanches, landslides, rock falls, mud flows, and unstable or potentially unstable slopes; and
2. Seismic effects.
